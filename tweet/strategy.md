# TracE — X Content Strategy

## Core Positioning
> A technical content writer who used AI to build his own productivity app — and actually uses it daily.

**Identity on X:** Someone who uses AI to move fast and ship real things. Not a developer. Not a guru. Just someone who identified a gap, used the right tools, and closed it.

---

## Phases

### Phase 1 — Basement & Testing (Now)
- **Goal:** Find what resonates. Build the first 50–100 followers organically.
- **Tone:** Personal, honest, slightly vulnerable. No brand talk. No company tags.
- **Topics:** AI-assisted building, personal productivity, "writer who builds"
- **Platform hint:** No backend/stack details. No company handles tagged.
- **Frequency:** 3x per week max. Quality over noise.
- **Success signal:** Any post breaking 500 impressions or getting 5+ replies.

### Phase 2 — Traction (When Phase 1 stabilises)
- Introduce stack details more openly
- Start referencing the backend, tools used, architecture decisions
- Begin tagging relevant community handles
- Build-in-public cadence: 1 technical post per week

### Phase 3 — Authority (When 500+ followers, consistent engagement)
- Tag company handles openly
- Collaborative posts, quote tweets, community building
- Position as the "AI builder" voice in the developer/writer crossover space

---

## Content Types

| Type | Use when | Cadence |
|------|----------|---------|
| Thread | Origin story, build updates, lessons | 1x per 2 weeks |
| Single tweet | Insight, take, relatable moment | 2x per week |
| Engagement tweet | Poll, question, open prompt | 1x per week (mixed in) |

---

## Rules

1. **No Catalyst/company mentions in Phase 1.** Grow first, then leverage.
2. **Every thread must end with a follow hook** — give readers a reason to press follow *now*.
3. **Lead with the human story.** Data and tech always come second.
4. **Real numbers always.** "31 days", "1 month", "5 features" — specificity builds trust.
5. **280 chars per tweet** (non-premium). Every tweet must fit. No exceptions.
6. **Threads = multiple connected 280-char tweets.** Labelled clearly in files.
7. **Keep records here.** After each post, log the traction in the tracking section below.
